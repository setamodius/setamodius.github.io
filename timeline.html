Timeline

An evolution, not a list of achievements.

Early electronics

Taking apart electronics and building small circuits out of curiosity — the first hands-on contact with how systems actually work underneath their surface.

University years

Formal study alongside self-driven projects — early programming, microcontrollers, and the first real exposure to systems engineering as a discipline rather than a hobby.

First SCADA projects

Entry into industrial software: working close to the hardware, reading field protocols, and learning what "production" means when the system being monitored is a physical plant, not a web request.

International tunnel deployments

On-site installation and commissioning of SCADA and automation systems for tunnel and transportation projects abroad — integration challenges, remote construction sites, and direct exposure to how systems behave outside the comfort of a controlled lab.

Transition to distributed architectures

Moving SCADA platforms from monolithic applications to microservices, MQTT-based communication, and containerized deployment — driven by the operational limits of the old architecture, not by trend-following.

Current: AI and automation

Applying the same instinct that drove the earlier transitions — understand the system, then simplify it — to artificial intelligence and where it intersects with industrial automation and distributed infrastructure.
